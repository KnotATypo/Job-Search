Raytheon Australia is a trusted capability partner to the Australian Defence Force, providing technology solutions that protect and secure our sovereign projects.

Right now, the company is experiencing exciting growth across the country, and we are looking for smart and energised Cyber Compliance Analysts to join our team on the Australian Defence Air Traffic System (ADATS) program.

ADATS is concerned in providing maintenance, engineering, program management and logistics support services to sustain each of the 14 Air Traffic Management sites, including control sites, radars, and other equipment vital for safeguarding Australia’s military airspace.

Our range of interesting and unique projects leverages the latest technologies to ensure Australia’s superior defence capability continues to keep Australia safe.

You will join a local team and be backed with global expertise, as well as a tailored professional development program, to ensure you and your work are successful.

As a Cyber Compliance Analyst for the ADATS program, your responsibilities include:

  • Providing technical leadership and direction for all cybersecurity related activities.
  • Leading the activities required to achieve and maintain customer security accreditation for the identified ADATS systems.
  • Developing and maintaining key security documents on behalf of the customer for the identified ADATS systems.
  • Assessing engineering design changes from a cybersecurity perspective. And in consultation with the customer undertake all identified cyber security activities as required.
  • Liaise, engage, and maintain close contact with customers, cyber security stakeholders and relevant agencies.

To excel in this role, you should have:

  • A University Degree in Information Technology, or equivalent experience.
  • A minimum of 4 years of prior relevant experience.
  • Advanced knowledge of the cybersecurity.
  • Optional professional certifications such as Certified Information Systems Security Professional (CISSP), Global Information Assurance Certification (GIAC), or Certified in Risk and Information Systems Control (CRISC).

Successful candidates must be eligible to obtain and maintain a Baseline Security clearance as needed. Eligibility can be found at
