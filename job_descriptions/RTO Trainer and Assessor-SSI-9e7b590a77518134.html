About the Role
We are seeking TAE Qualified Trainer and Assessors to provide high quality, professional, culturally responsive training and assessment services to build the capabilities of our students and the employers that we work. this will be achieved by planning, developing, delivering and reviewing training and assessment in line with the Australian Skills Quality Authority (ASQA), State Training Regulators and Authorities, State and Federal Government contracts and guidelines, Training Packages and all other organisational guidelines and requirements.

Responsibilities, Accountabilities and Deliverables

  • Deliver outstanding training and assessment, that is of high quality, engaging, industry current and culturally responsive in vocational discipline in a classroom environment and/or on-the-job and/or online, in accordance with agreed schedules and training plans
  • Observe and monitor student training and assessment progression, with purposeful follow-up and advise the Training Manager with a solutions focused outcome
  • Manage and ensure attainment of position and contract KPI’s, milestones and outcomes, ensuring Student and Stakeholder satisfaction, are achieved.
  • Provide a learning environment that is supportive and culturally responsive, that optimises successful outcomes for all students
  • Liaise and work with industry and employers to gain industry consultation, source student placements and establish current trends and market needs.
  • Ensure all administrative functions of the role are completed in a timely manner
  • Participate in the development, preparation, validation and moderation of training and assessment practice and materials and contribute to all and any continuous improvement activities.
  • Be responsible and accountable for maintaining own currency and professional development in industry (vocationally) and in VET (Vocational Education and Training)
Essential Criteria
You possess the TAE40116 Certificate IV in Training and Assessment (or its successor) Plus vocational qualifications to the level of or higher to that of the qualification delivered and assessed, we are particularly interested in Qualifications in; First Aid and CPR, CHC33015 – Certificate III in Individual Support.

  • Have Vocational Industry currency and experience relevant to qualification/s being delivered and assessed that is no older than 3 years
  • Can demonstrate current knowledge of the VET Sector, ASQA, Compliance Framework that governs Registered Training Organisations, State Training Funding Agreements
  • You are someone that enjoys their role, you have the ability to provide engaging and supportive training programs to vulnerable cohorts such as Culturally and Linguistically diverse, Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islanders, Migrants and Refugees, Students with a Disability, Long Term Unemployed that obtain successful outcomes
