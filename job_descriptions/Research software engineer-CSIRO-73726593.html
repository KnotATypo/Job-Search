About our team

Our team uses a combination of maths, computer models and datasets to test, develop and harmonized fundamental concepts and methods that underpin biosecurity science. We are interested in the intersection of science and policy in biosecurity. We are a diverse team, composed of ecologist, social scientist, and software developers that routinely interact with policy makers to achieve better science-based outcomes in biosecurity applied mainly to market access and phytosanitary measures (i.e. quarantine and biosecurity measures).

We routinely interact with scientists, policy makers, and industry stakeholders. The team is part of the Pest Management team, within Health and Biosecurity, mostly located in Brisbane.

Expectations and Summary of Role

This role sits within the biosecurity area, you will bring engineering capability focusing on developing and maintaining science-based tools that are practical, robust and can be applied to a wide range of biosecurity and market access challenges. You will work closely with a diverse team of scientists, other engineers and UX designers to develop the biosecurity software systems. You will bring best practices in software development and data management to the science that is developed and delivered within our biosecurity focus team.  This will include:

  • Establishing robust and reproducible data workflows.
  • Working closely with scientists to design, create and maintain standardise and reproducible code for mathematical and statistical analyses related to biosecurity.
  • Working closely with UX designers and scientists to design, create and test prototype web-applications for industry and government partners.

Experience in biosecurity is not necessary for this role.

Job Duties and Responsibilities

Contribute to the digital transformation of biosecurity research and delivery by improving the data management, application of data science, software development practices, and the rapid prototyping of solutions, including data visualization interfaces. For example:

  1. Assist in the creation and maintenance of rigorous data- and code- management practices, including thorough documentation and version control
  2. Maintain and run ETL pipelines, workflows and scripts
  3. Writing reusable code (e.g. packages or libraries) for biosecurity analyses
  4. Writing of production-grade user interfaces in collaboration with UX designers and scientists
  5. Verification and validation of both software and data pipelines
  6. Keeping detailed records of development process
  7. Clearly and regularly communicate with scientist and UX designers involved in software development projects
