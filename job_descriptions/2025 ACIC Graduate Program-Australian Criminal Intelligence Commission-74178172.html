About the program

As a graduate at the ACIC you will be at the forefront of tackling transnational serious and organised crime affecting Australia. The work you will do, directly contributes to keep the Australian community safe.


You will have the opportunity to be a part of Australia’s National Intelligence Community and work alongside some of the country’s most innovative and effective intelligence and law enforcement professionals.


Our 10-month program will provide you an opportunity to develop your professional skills and hone your tradecraft, in a diverse and supportive agency. We offer a number of benefits including:

  • various rotations that will expose you to a variety of different capabilities within the ACIC
  • comprehensive development program aligned to your role, including a mix of on-the-job and formal learning


Upon successful completion of our graduate program you will be placed into a ongoing position, with a salary at APS4.3 as per Schedule 1 of the ACIC Section 24(1) Determination and Pay Scale 2022


Please note, roles will be offered in Canberra with the possibility of positions being offered in Brisbane, Melbourne or Sydney.


Who are we looking for?

We are looking for qualified graduates who are driven and passionate about the important work we do. We want graduates who are problem solvers, innovative thinkers and committed to making a difference.


We are seeking graduates with degrees in the following fields:

  • Corporate: human resources, finance and accounting, legal, communication and media, and project management, psychology
  • Intelligence: criminal justice, criminology, international relations, national security, telecommunications and engineering
  • Technical: data science, data analytics, cyber security, digital forensics, network engineering, computer programming, coding and computer science


Essential qualifications

  • Due to complete your undergraduate by the end of 2024 or have completed an undergraduate degree within the last 3 years.


Eligibility

  • Citizenship – To be eligible for employment with the ACIC, applicants must be an Australian citizen.
  • Security Clearance - The Australian community expects ACIC employees to maintain the highest levels of security and integrity. Successful applicants will be required to obtain and maintain a minimum Negative Vetting 1 security clearance.
  • Pre-Employment Screening – All candidates must satisfy an Organisational Suitability Assessment (OSA) prior to being offered employment at the ACIC.
  • Vaccination Status – To be eligible to work at the ACIC, you must be fully vaccinated against COVID-19.


The ACIC is committed to diversity and inclusion . We celebrate and champion diversity to ensure we reflect the community we serve, and pride ourselves on our vibrant culture. We welcome applications from Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples, women, people with disability, people from culturally and linguistically diverse backgrounds, those who identify as LGBTIQ+, neurodiverse, mature aged employees and carers.


More information about our conditions is available in the ACIC Enterprise Agreement 2016–2019 and Determination 2022/1 on our website .
