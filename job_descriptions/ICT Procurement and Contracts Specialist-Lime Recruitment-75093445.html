Lime Recruitment is currently recruiting an ICT Procurement and Contracts Specialist contract for 6 months with possible extension based in the Brisbane CBD Brisbane and working from home.

Procurement responsibilities:

  • Contribute to effective end-to-end procurement processes for the ICT category, including supply market analysis, procurement planning, tendering, evaluation methodologies, supplier negotiation, and contracting in collaboration with relevant University staff.
  • Review Procurement submissions including procurement plans, procurement exemptions, evaluation reports to ensure they meet the procurement policy and principles.
  • Independently prepare ICT procurement contracts based on the specifications and approved template terms and where necessary Independently prepare straightforward variations to ICT procurement contracts based on the approved templates.
  • Support IT Managers by providing support in writing ICT specification and scope of work.
  • Conduct pricing analysis and commercial modelling during tender evaluation and assist in decision making.
  • Publish sourcing events through the approved tendering platform and monitor and update as necessary.

Contracts responsibilities:

  • Work cooperatively with procurement stakeholders to understand transaction requirements and develop the best approach to contract terms.
  • Administer complex contracts in collaboration with Contract Managers
  • Contribute effectively to all aspects of, and where appropriate lead, supplier negotiations, including commercial and contract negotiation.
  • Review supplier contracts for compliance and business risk management.
  • Liaise with the Legal Services in relation to identified legal risks and, variation, contract breach and termination actions associated with managed contracts.
  • Maintain efficient records management practices utilising appropriate tools.
  • Adopt and promote sound cyber security practices in compliance with the Cyber Security Policy and according to cyber security procedures and standards developed by the University or Division
  • Develop and maintain positive and constructive relationships and feedback mechanisms with internal and external clients, vendors, and staff to represent procurement services and ensure responsiveness to current and future needs.
