Key Responsibilities:

As the Principal Specialist - Development Assessment and Engineering, your key responsibilities will include:
  • Providing expert leadership and management to the Development Assessment, Development Engineering, and Business Support teams, ensuring compliance with legislative requirements, Council's Corporate Plan, Operational Plan, and Planning Schemes.
  • Exercising strategic oversight to guarantee the professional and timely execution of programs, reports, and site assessments.
  • Formulating and updating policies, operational guidelines, and providing input to the strategic planning team to ensure best practices.
  • Preparing advice and fostering positive relationships with Local, State, and Federal Government Departments, as well as other Statutory Authorities, in accordance with development legislation.
  • Developing comprehensive strategies in the planning domain that align with broader Council initiatives, delivering high-quality outcomes for the Council and its residents.
  • Providing professional advice to the multi-disciplinary professionals involved in development assessments.
  • Promoting and implementing new initiatives and best practice solutions.
  • Ensuring strict adherence to Council policies, procedures, and instructions, with a focus on delivering high-quality and safe services.
  • Maintaining accountability and ensuring appropriate use of information systems, in compliance with record-keeping requirements.

Formal Qualifications/Technical Skills:
  • Tertiary undergraduate or postgraduate qualification in Urban and Regional Planning (or similar) recognized by the Planning Institute of Australia.
  • Desirable qualifications in Management, Law, Administration, or Environmental Science.
  • Current Qld C Class driver's license.
  • Highly developed time management, negotiation, and problem-solving skills.
  • Demonstrated management skills with the ability to motivate and lead staff in diverse activities.
  • Strong written and verbal communication skills, including computer literacy, report writing, research, and coordination skills.

Knowledge:
  • Extensive knowledge of the Planning Act, other related planning and environment legislation, and planning schemes.
  • Understanding of other Council and State Department programs and activities and their impact on Town Planning issues in the Council area.

Experience:
  • High-level experience in delivering development assessment within a medium to large and diverse environment.
  • High-level experience in leadership within a dynamic and politically sensitive environment.
