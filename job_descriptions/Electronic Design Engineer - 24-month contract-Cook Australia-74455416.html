An exciting opportunity for you to work on projects which support Cook Medical’s electromedical devices in the market. You will be responsible for diagnosing software/firmware, hardware and networking issues, proposing solutions, verification and validation of design changes and transferring design changes to manufacture.

Reporting to the Team Leader – Post Market Engineering, you will bring these skills and your design control background to a collaborative, high performing team who are constantly looking at ways to evolve and improve the way we work.

At Cook Medical, we understand that unique perspectives, ideas and contributions only strengthen our workforce and encourage innovation through solving problems together.

Key Responsibilities:

  • Management of supply chain related design change activities for our electromedical devices.
  • Review of product performance and safety, including root cause analysis and design solutions to resolve any identified problems.
  • Management engineering driven projects or programs.
  • Effective communication of technical analyses and risk assessments to cross-functional stakeholders.

To succeed in this role, you must have the following:

  • Bachelor’s degree in electronic engineering or equivalent.
  • Experience using Altium Designer for circuit capture and printed circuit board (PCB) layout.
  • Experience transferring PCB assembly designs to manufacture.
  • Experience with embedded control and programming in C.
  • Knowledge and experience in the application of design controls.
  • Knowledge and Experience in the application of risk management techniques.
  • Highly developed technical writing skills and attention for detail.

Preferred candidates shall also have demonstrated experience in the following:

  • Medical device manufacturing environment or other highly regulated industry.
  • Application of and compliance to the medical electrical device standard (IEC 60601-1) or similar product standards.
  • Design of software for Ethernet connected devices (TCP/IP).
