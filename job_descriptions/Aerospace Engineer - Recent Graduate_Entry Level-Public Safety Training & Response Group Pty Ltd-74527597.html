Public Safety Training & Response Group is an independent, Australian veteran owned company helping people and organisations deal with an extensive range of emergency situations and delivering industry relevant and nationally accredited public safety training and response certifications.

We save lives early with a highly resourced and respected team of responders and educators who provide specialised solutions to avert or mitigate crisis.

Your role:

Our growing team is seeking a highly motivated individual to join our team as an Aerospace Engineer.

This role will suit recent graduates looking for an entry level position working in the Remotely Piloted Aircraft Systems (RPAS) sector.

This newly created full-time role will provide you with the opportunity to perform maintenance and engineering work associated with the design, development, integration and modification of a range of RPAS (drones/UAV).

Responsibilities:

The main responsibilities of this role include, but are not limited to:

  • Maintain airworthiness of RPAS and serviceability of specialist equipment, including payload and ground control stations.
  • Perform engineering work associated with the design, development, integration, and modification of Remotely Piloted Aircraft Systems (RPAS).
  • Ensure functionality and reliability of each sub-system by performing tests and validation.
  • Analyse and troubleshoot problems related to RPAS and propose solutions to optimise performance.
  • Identify opportunities to improve existing systems.
  • Develop and maintain technical documentation, including system specifications, test procedures and operations manual.
  • Perform other administrative duties and responsibilities as required to support our RPAS operations.
  • Continuing professional development in engineering skills and emerging technologies.

Requirements:

  • Recent graduate in Engineering, trade qualification or relevant verifiable experience in Aerospace, Electrical, Electronic or other relevant discipline.
  • Practical skills in electrical/electronic projects, including wiring, soldering, and handling powered tools.
  • Ability to interpret and create technical documentation, including electrical diagrams and schematics.
  • Familiar with handling DC power systems and lithium-based batteries.
  • Experience in 3D-modelling using CAD software and additive manufacturing.
  • Experience in using microcontrollers/microcomputers (Arduino, ESP-32, Raspberry Pi).
  • Basic programming skills in C language.
  • Able to work permanently in Australia.
  • Occasional travel within Australia.

Desirable:

  • CASA Remote Pilot License (RePL).
  • Experience in the aviation / unmanned system industry.
  • Experience in building and flying drones, RC vehicles or similar.
  • Familiar with using flight controllers and configuring Ardupilot systems.
  • Familiar with Linux systems and Python programming.
  • Familiar with CASA regulation in relation to RPAS operations.
  • Experience or have an interest in training delivery.
