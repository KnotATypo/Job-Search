FindStaff are seeking a Mobile Provisional Psychologist to work in our new division at Mas National…

About us

Mas is proudly part of the IntoWork Australia Group, the leading national provider of Employment, Skills and Support services that support economic and social participation. Everything we do, including our range of tailored pathways for individuals and workforce solutions for employers, is underpinned by our vision - 'Vibrant Futures' - for our staff, service users and business clients.

Mas exists to create and support workforce employment, development and growth for individuals, employers and communities.

We offer transition, mentoring, networking and career services across Australia. We are an Australian Apprenticeship Support Network (AASN) provider delivering with our partners across QLD, SA, TAS and VIC, and deliver Workforce Australia Services for IntoJobs across NSW, QLD, SA, VIC, TAS and WA.



About the role

The Mobile Provisional Psychologist, working in a national team, will provide one to one and group Allied Health supports to individuals in employment and vocational education services. You will provide predominately face to face servicing with 1:1 and group counselling services to Employment Services participants across the Brisbane area.

This is a flexible role with part time permanent, casual or contract opportunities available.

Key Responsibilities include:

  • Provide face-to-face and Telehealth Allied Health consultations to individuals and groups.
  • Ensure governance framework is adhered to with regards to service provision and privacy
  • Providing individualised supports, connecting to local networks, supporting the individuals' interventional supports
  • Produce appropriate documentation and reporting relevant to services delivered, including utilisation of system databases.
  • Deliver outcome-based performance to continue to grow service utilisation.
  • Work in conjunction with service delivery teams, supporting staff to identify demand for non-vocational interventional support.
  • Engage in Professional Development opportunities and registration requirements to ensure continuous development of role capabilities.



About you:

To be successful in this role, you must have a passion for the delivery of Allied Health services to support pre-employment and in work participants.

You will be able to demonstrate experience in a similar role including:

  • Previous experience in delivery of Allied Health Services, preferably within the employment services industry
  • Experience in the design and delivery of contemporary group and individual support programs to promote progress to, and in employment.
  • Willingness to engage in continuous development of profession and support national service delivery team.
  • Strong administrative skills, with keen attention to detail
  • Understanding and demonstrated experience working in a clinical governance environment.

Education & Accreditation

The successful incumbent of this role must meet the following:

  • Qualified Psychologist who is currently registered as a Psychologist or Provisional Psychologist with the Australian Health Practitioner Regulation Agency (AHPRA)


Applicants must be eligible to work in Australia. National Police Check & Working with Children checks are required and can be arranged for the successful applicant.
