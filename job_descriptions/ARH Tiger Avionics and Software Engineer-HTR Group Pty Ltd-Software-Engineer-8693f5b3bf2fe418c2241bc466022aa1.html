We have a current opportunity for an ARH Tiger Avionics and Software Engineer , on a contract basis within the Defence sector. The role is based in Brisbane, QLD . The contract is for a 36 month period, with no extension option.

Start: circa 15 May 2024
DSS Skill Set: Level 3 - Specialist Engineering - Aerospace Systems

The ARH Tiger Avionics and Software Engineer will be responsible for, but not limited to the following duties:

  • Finalise Transition of the ARH Type Records to a DASA standard including Type Certification Basis, Type
  • Certification Data Sheet & Statement of Operating Intent and Usage - including the development of any new MCRIs.
  • Maintain local procedures including record keeping systems to enable the conduct of DOSA obligations.
  • Review and contribute to AwIPs and Major Changes approvals - the latter including CPPs and MCRIs.
  • Review ADPs provided at key points of the CM lifecycle, in support of AASPO meeting CASG Technical Acceptance requirements.
  • Conduct oversight of MTCH obligations conducted by Airbus AP IAW DASR 21.A.44 established under contractual arrangements. Monitor the effectiveness of the Contract for AAP to deliver MTCH obligations conducted by Airbus AP IAW DASR 21.A.44.
  • Assist with audits and assurance review of Airbus AP technical product as part of the wider AASPO performance assurance program.
  • Training and mentoring support for AASPO Tiger Avionics and Software Engineering Staff - DASR, aircraft certification, Avionics and Software, in context of the current and historical ARH Tiger program.

Minimum mandatory requirements:

1. AGSVA Security clearance
2. Demonstrated skill, experience and qualifications:

  • Discipline - Platform Engineering
  • Services - Electrical/Power, Sensor Management, EWSP Airborne, Avionics Systems, Communications Systems, Software Systems and Aerospace Systems
  • Lead Skill Level - Level 3/4 with demonstrated experience in the application of DASR
  • Demonstrate previous experience a level of competency to achieve the required level of Authority from the CENGR IAW AASPO-SOP-(ENG) 12-0-135.

3. Demonstrated skills, experience and ability to perform Tiger Avionics and Software Engineering including:

  • Conduct CCB Engineering reviews of ADPs to support Development Approval, IA and SR recommendations
  • Compliance sampling of ADPs
  • Engineering investigations (small / large)
  • Progression of Tiger DACM priorities
  • Review and Revise Engineering and Business Practices
  • Provision of SME advice to Weapons System/Capability Management activities
  • Performance of compliance, quality assurance, governance oversight and corporate reporting activities
  • Compliance Finding activities
  • Development and delivery of engineering training modules as required
  • Provision of engineering mentoring services
  • Provision of handovers and disengagement
  • Provision of Project Management of Services and report on progress
  • Effective relationship building with team members, management and stakeholders.

4. Ability to research and formulate a strategy with respect to the Deliverables.
