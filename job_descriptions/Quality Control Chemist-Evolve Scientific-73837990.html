About the Company:

Our client is a leading Australian radiopharmaceutical manufacturer, who are eager to expand their Brisbane team with a new QC Chemist.

About the Role:
As a QC Chemist, reporting to the Facility Manager, you will be responsible for product manufacturing, analytical testing of in-process and finished products via HPLC, GC and classical wet chemistry techniques, as well as providing support with data analysis, reporting and documentation review/development.

Due to the nature of the business, the successful candidate must be available to work early mornings with shifts starting at 5am Monday through Friday.

Duties:
  • Perform Quality Control tests and equipment calibrations in line with documented processes and procedures;
  • Attend to preparation and management of reference standards and reagents;
  • Attend to data analysis and reporting against product specifications, and ensuring the accuracy and completeness of all records;
  • Assist with documentation development and review;
  • Perform validation of new and existing equipment, products and processes where necessary;
  • Troubleshoot and perform scheduled equipment maintenance;
  • Conduct and support investigations regarding out of Trend (OOT)/Out of specification (OOS);
  • Act as an Authorised Person – Release for Use.

Skills & Experience:
To be considered for the QC Chemist position candidates must have:
  • A science or pharmaceutical degree (ideally chemistry);
  • Attention to detail and accuracy with an ability to follow standard operating procedures;
  • Good interpersonal skills that will allow for strong collaboration within a team working environment;
  • Strong organisational skills with the ability to work within deadlines;
  • Ability to work without direct supervision;
  • Quick learner with the ability to adapt to new tasks and systems;
  • Strong verbal and written communication skills;
  • Experience in HPLC and/or UPLC method development and optimisation of test procedures, including complex samples (minimum 2.5 years);
  • Enjoys hands on laboratory work;
  • Computer skills: Microsoft Office (Word, Excel, Powerpoint);
  • Proactive approach to problem-solving and issue resolution;
  • Previous experience drawn from Pharmaceutical (GMP) or a related industry (desirable).
