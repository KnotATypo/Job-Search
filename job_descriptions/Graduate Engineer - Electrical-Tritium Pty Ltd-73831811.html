About Tritium

Founded in 2001, Tritium (NASDAQ: DCFC) designs and manufactures proprietary hardware and software to create advanced and reliable DC fast chargers for electric vehicles. Tritium’s compact and robust chargers are designed to look great on Main Street and thrive in harsh conditions, through technology engineered to be easy to install, own, and use. Tritium is focused on continuous innovation in support of our customers around the world.

About the Role

This position reports directly to the Engineering Manager - Systems and Electrical, at Tritium PTY Ltd and is based at Murarrie, QLD. The Graduate Electrical Engineer has responsibility for ownership, delivery and documentation of assigned electrical systems form initial concept, verification, and through into production.

The role is part of the Systems and Electrical engineering team, part of the broader new product development. The position is ideal for a recent graduate seeking to gain valuable experience in electrical design within the context of high-power systems for electric vehicle fast chargers. For the right candidate, there are opportunities to work on high power DC electrical systems, power electronics conversion systems, ELV electrical and control systems and more.

Key responsibilities for this role include, but are not limited to:
  • Understand requirements to develop electrical schematics, drawings, and documentation, working closely with systems engineers, harness engineers, and electrical engineers.
  • Supporting power systems simulation, fault studies, protection design and coordination, and component selection of high power AC and DC control gear and switchgear.
  • Contributing to product development processes such as Requirements Development, DFMEA’s, DVP’s, and risk assessment activities.
  • Collaborating within a cross-functional team to ensure insulation coordination and safe power circuit routing within the product.
  • Assisting in the development of documentation to support certification and testing activities, including single-line diagrams and sample site designs.
  • Supporting the execution of test plans to ensure coverage against a broad range of electrical fault scenarios and to ensure product compliance.
  • Collaborating to ensure design solutions are appropriate and meet cost, safety, schedule requirements, and applicable industry standards.
  • Assisting in managing key technical supplier relationships to monitor industry trends and support the specification of key componentry.
  • Be personally motivated to look for opportunities to up-skill and further improve knowledge and experience
