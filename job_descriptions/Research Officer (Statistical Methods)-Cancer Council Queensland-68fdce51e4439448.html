Research Officer (Statistical methods)

About Us

For over 60 years, Cancer Council Queensland’s pioneering work has helped save thousands of lives and has improved the quality of life for people living with cancer. Our mission is to lead Queenslanders in a partnership against cancer.

Cancer Council Queensland raises funds that are dedicated to improving the quality of life for people living with cancer through research to support prevention and early detection.

We are dynamic, outcome-focused, responsive to community needs, and committed to voluntarism and the pursuit of excellence.

Cancer Council Queensland employees and volunteers lead Queenslanders in a partnership against cancer by employing high-performing employees who live our values of being responsible, inclusive, equitable, and collaborative.

Working at Cancer Council Queensland

Our greatest asset is our diverse team of talented, savvy people. Our awesome employees and volunteers tell us that Cancer Council Queensland is a meaningful, rewarding and fun place to work.

We provide a positive and supportive working environment where you will be a valued team member. We love to recognise and reward our team of superstars with employee benefits such as extra paid days off.

Our research

At Cancer Council Queensland, we conduct a collaborative program of research in cancer control. It is focused on understanding cancer risk, ensuring early detection of cancer, improving cancer survivorship and reducing inequities in cancer outcomes through population cancer surveillance.

The Viertel Cancer Research Centre is a multi-disciplinary research centre with strengths in four key areas – cancer epidemiology, childhood cancer research, health services and behavioural research. We are particularly interested in identifying and addressing challenges related to social and geographic inequities in cancer-related outcomes.

About the role

The Research Officer (Statistical methods) works within the Descriptive Epidemiology research team and develops statistical methods that better describe and understand spatial patterns of cancer in Australia. Ideally suited to a recent postdoctoral graduate (academic level A or B), the role will provide opportunities to develop and apply statistical methods, prepare manuscripts for publication in Q1 peer-reviewed journals, and collaborate with our university partners.

This role is fixed term (2 years) and full-time (37.5 hours per week).

About you

To be successful in this role, you will be able to learn, apply and critically assess statistical methods for epidemiology.

Qualifications, Experience, Knowledge and Skills

Essential:
  • A PhD or equivalent experience.
  • Qualifications in statistical, mathematical or epidemiological modelling with demonstrated skills in statistical software programming with R, python and/or Stata, and data management skills.
  • Ability to learn and apply new statistical and epidemiological methods.
  • Demonstrated spoken and written communication skills.
  • Demonstrated organisational skills and ability to conduct work to a high level of accuracy.
  • Ability to work independently whilst maintaining a strong working relationship with the research team.
Desirable:
  • Peer-reviewed publications as lead author.
  • Demonstrated experience working with sensitive research data.
  • Demonstrated experience conducting statistical modelling in the Bayesian framework.
  • Demonstrated experience in epidemiology, particularly cancer epidemiology.
