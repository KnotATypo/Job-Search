About NIOA

NIOA is a family-owned global munitions company. Our operations include NIOA Australia, NIOA New Zealand, the Australian Missile Corporation (AMC), Barrett Firearms (USA) and joint venture company Rheinmetall NIOA Munitions.

About the Role

Ensure the confidentiality, integrity and availability of NIOA’s data and information environment by proactively addressing cyber security threats and mitigating risks, managing incident operational response and recovery, and continuously improving cyber maturity and overall security posture.

Key Responsibilities

  • Manage all aspects of day-to-day cyber operations for business units within the Australian and New Zealand (AU NZ) region.
  • Act as the cyber point of contact for all staff and service providers.
  • Develop and deliver cyber awareness training for all staff.
  • Lead the operational response during cyber incidents, undertake annual incident response testing, continuously improve the IRP.
  • Proactively identify and remediate cyber weaknesses, and continuously reduce vulnerabilities and related risk.
  • Support the development and deployment of internal cyber security policies, procedures etc.
  • Contribute to security architecture planning activities.
  • Provide information security advice to the business, including evaluating new systems, software, and services.
  • Represent NIOA’s interests at Defence industry meetings and events.
  • Deliver operational cyber reporting for the Information Security Committee (ISC), and senior stakeholders as required.

Experience and Qualifications

  • Bachelor's degree in computer science, information technology, cybersecurity, or a related field.
  • 5+ years’ experience in cyber operations, including incident management.
  • Relevant professional qualifications are desirable, such as Certified Information Systems Security Professional (CISSP) or similar.
  • Developing and delivering cyber awareness training to increase cyber security maturity and organisational preparedness.
  • Identifying and remediating cyber risks and threats in a complex environment.
  • Evaluating, implementing and administering cyber security systems and services.

Personal Attributes

  • Strong analytical and problem-solving skills.
  • Excellent verbal and written communication skills.
  • High-level attention to detail.
  • Be effective working both autonomously and collaboratively.
  • Resourcefulness and excellent time management skills.
