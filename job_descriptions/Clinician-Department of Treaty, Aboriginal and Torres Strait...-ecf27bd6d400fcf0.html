Full job description

The key responsibilities of the Clinician are:
  • Participate as a member of the clinical services team to develop, implement and review (consistent with specialist discipline):
  • client assessments including assessments of risk.
  • individual plans.
  • treatment / rehabilitation programs in the facility and out in the community.
  • transition plans from the secure facility back to the community.
  • Plan, implement and evaluate treatment and rehabilitation programs and interventions that increase positive behaviours, reduce offending behaviours and behaviours of concern, improve quality of life and work towards a transition back to the community.
  • Work with Operational Service Delivery Teams in identifying and acting on opportunities for clients to develop skills and competencies.
  • Effectively respond to and support clients to develop positive alternatives to engaging in offending and challenging behaviour. This will include undertake functional behaviour assessments for clients who exhibit frequent behaviours of harm.
  • Actively seek and negotiate opportunities with local community organisations, sport and recreation clubs and educational and vocational providers to support clients to maximise access for clients to a range of opportunities.
  • Support clients to access the community including overnight Limited Community Treatment, and link with supports and resources in their own community with a view to transfer and transition.
  • Monitor, evaluate and review the intervention strategies against the person's needs and goals according to current disability service standards.
  • Conduct assessments, write and review reports, individual plans, undertake administrative tasks and data collection as required and contribute to the development of court and tribunal submissions.
  • Ensure the safety of clients, staff and visitors and the security of the facility including observations and searches, escort duties and duress response duties, and participate in the management of challenging and aggressive behaviour.
  • It is vital Clinicians have the ability to be emotionally resilient and demonstrate perseverance in achieving objectives and coping effectively with setbacks, challenges and incidents.
  • Adhere to policies, procedures and guidelines that drive clinical governance and multidisciplinary practice, work within the legislative framework that governs the forensic disability service, and participate in identifying, developing and implementing continuous improvement activities to improve service delivery.
  • Participate in individual time/supervision and professional development provided by a Clinical Team Leader to ensure ongoing development and provision of high-quality services to clients.
  • Provide information, support and training to staff (within FDS), family and other support agencies, from a holistic approach in relation to skill development, particularly with regard to individual clients.
  • Adhere to clinical decisions made by the Clinical Team Leader, and follow direction provided by members of the Senior Leadership Team.
  • Provide clinical follow up and support to client's pre-admission and post transition.
Mandatory requirement:
Registered degree qualifications in Allied Health (Psychology, Speech and Language Pathology, Occupational Therapy) or Human Services, Social Work or Behaviour Sciences.
Applications to remain current for 12 months.
