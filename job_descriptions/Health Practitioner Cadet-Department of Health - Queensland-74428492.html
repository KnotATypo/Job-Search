Cadets are employed as a temporary part time employee to gain experience during their 12 weeks (456 hours) of work placement which is scheduled to fit around their full-time study commitments during the calendar year. The cadet will work under the delegation and supervision of the Skills Development Unit within Pathology Queensland.

The successful candidate will perform basic duties under the direction of your health practitioner, with the quality of work output closely assessed.

Role fit

The essential requirements for this role are.

Clinical Ability:

  • Willingness to develop clinical competency in Pathology and participate in work output assessment and feedback processes.
  • Ability to work under direction and within the limitations of a defined role in patient care.
  • Demonstrated ability to effectively use computers and general office equipment and to quickly acquire ability to use new information and data systems.

Team Participation/Communication:

  • Demonstrated ability to communicate effectively and work collaboratively as part of a team with a good understanding of own scope of practice in a complex and demanding work environment.
  • Demonstrated skills in self-organisation and initiative to enable the timely completion of a variety of tasks allowing efficient service function.
Reporting/work relationships
  • Cadets report operationally to a line manager and professionally to a supervising Health Practitioner from their discipline.
  • Cadets will be supported culturally through being networked with other Aboriginal and/or Torres Strait Islander Cadets, and through a local Aboriginal and/or Torres Strait Islander mentor.
  • This position has no direct line management responsibility or financial delegations.

Mandatory qualifications, registrations and other requirements

  • Must be enrolled full time (defined as at least 75% of the full-time study load) at an Australian university located in Australia, in a Bachelor of Medical Laboratory science.
  • be an Australian resident.

How to apply

Please provide the following information to the panel to assess your suitability:

  1. A short-written response (maximum 1-2 pages, dot points acceptable) on how your experience, abilities, knowledge and personal qualities would enable you to achieve the key responsibilities and meet the skill requirements of the role.
  2. Your current CV or resume, including two referees who have a thorough knowledge of your capabilities, work performance and conduct within the previous two years. It is preferable to include your current, immediate or past supervisor.
  3. Late applications cannot be submitted online. For a late application to be considered, please arrange approval and submission via the contact person.
  4. Applications will remain current for 12 months or for the duration of the vacancy. Future vacancies of a temporary, full time and part time nature may also be filled through this recruitment process.
  5. Only those persons eligible to work in Australia may be employed by Queensland Health. Prospective employees are required to provide proof of identity and documentary evidence of their right to work in Australia.
