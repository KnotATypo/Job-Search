Full job description

Work options: Hybrid


The successful candidate will have the ability to work within a remote and an office environment.


This role will see you enhance your capabilities and the reputation of WSP’s Mine Waste team. You will collaborate with talented professionals working on iconic projects to drive future-ready outcomes that will benefit clients and communities.

As a Project Tailings Engineer based in our Brisbane office, you will join an industry-recognised team who's mission is to be at the cutting edge of the work we do. Enjoy a strong team culture and a team that promotes cross collaboration.

At WSP you’ll find the scale and reach you need to do the kind of work the world needs – and the culture and people that make it the best work of your life.

Mining

Our global team of over 5,200 dedicated mining professionals are supporting the industry to decarbonise mining operations in the accelerating green transition. Working collaboratively across borders and disciplines, you’ll join a leading team working on interesting and complex projects. We are experts in our field, and we are passionate about mining.


What you’ll do:

  • Travelling to site to oversee construction of tailings storage facility designs developed by our Mine Waste team;
  • Interpreting results from laboratory testing and preparing summary reports;
  • Developing a basis for defensible soil strength parameters, using laboratory and field results;
  • Undertaking screening level and detailed design analyses to support the design of tailings storage facilities and other earth structures, initially under close guidance from our senior specialists, but with a view to carrying out such work independently;
  • Developing designs for tailings storage facilities and other earth structures;
  • Managing the specification and implementing design changes while on site;
  • Assist with project planning and preparation of technical deliverables;
  • Assisting with the coordination of client-contractor requests;
  • Preparing and delivering technical reports on time;
  • Undertaking design assessments, including stability and seepage analyses;
  • Preparation of technical deliverables such as proposals and reports;
  • Working with and providing support to other members of the group as required;
  • Working with other professionals in project teams;
  • Conduct general field and office assignments under supervision;
  • Undertaking site and QA/QC activities;
  • Identify safety hazards and report OH&S incidents;
  • Complete inductions and pre-requisites as required;
  • Conduct all work to satisfy all company, client and government conditions, policies, procedures, regulations and standards, as required; and
  • Other duties as required.

About you:

  • Bachelor’s degree in Civil Engineering or related discipline;
  • Basic analytical and problem-solving skills;
  • Excellent written and verbal communication skills;
  • Enthusiasm for fieldwork with practical experience;
  • Ability to work efficiently autonomously and in a team environment;
  • Good attention to detail and time management ability; and
  • Proficiency in Microsoft Office Suite.
