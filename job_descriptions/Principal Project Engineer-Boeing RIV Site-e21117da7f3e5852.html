The Opportunity
We are seeking a Principal Project Engineer to support the Battlespace Communication System (BCS) Program. BCS is a next generation networking product that is the centerpiece for multiple projects and campaigns. The BCS combines both developmental and off the shelf hardware and software to provide world leading communications capability for ADF personnel. It can be rapidly deployed from anywhere, and its modular design provides a flexible, scalable solution that can be customized for various mission needs.
This role will see you applying your skills to either our core product for Army, upgrading the capability to integrate with related maritime communications projects, or modernization of the joint air battlespace management system.
Responsibilities

  • Drive system analysis, modelling and associated risk and opportunity assessments to support the certification and accreditation of a variety of engineering solutions and processes.
  • Work with and guide a range of both internal and external stakeholders to establish a common understanding of the risks and opportunities of various engineering solutions and processes so that the best possible outcome is achieved, considering the quality, schedule and constraints for all stakeholders.
  • The ability to communicate and collaborate with a diverse team, in a conscientious and inclusive way.
  • Scope, review and develop engineering documentation and models.
  • Determine tests and evidence that validates the models and solutions for certification and accreditation purposes and ensures the high-level objectives and principles are met.
Experience/Qualifications

Essential:
  • Bachelor's Degree in an Engineering discipline, Information Technology related fields or equivalent industry experience.
  • Demonstrated leadership and stakeholder management skills.
  • Demonstrated skills in analyzing and modelling systems, engineering solutions and associated processes.
  • Demonstrated skills in assessing risks and opportunities both technical and business related. These skills could come from a number of backgrounds, including software, hardware, firmware, safety, security, etc and could include the use of risk modelling and assessment tools.
  • Demonstrated skills in system engineering processes and documentation
  • Applicants must be Australian Citizens to meet defence security requirements.
Desirable:
  • Experience within a Defence or complex engineering project environment at the system of systems level is desirable.
  • We invite a diverse range of people to apply, including people from outside of the defence industry.
  • Familiar with a range of standards, includes certification and accreditation approaches. These could be in fields like security, safety, human factors, etc.
  • Hands on experience developing engineering solutions, that have significant software, firmware and hardware components.
  • Experience with the challenges of developing and testing real time systems in a safe and secure manner.
