The Opportunity
We are looking for Software Engineers with experience in software-defined networking and the Linux network stack to support the Battlespace Communication System (BCS) Program. BCS is a next generation networking product that is the centerpiece for multiple projects and campaigns. The BCS combines both developmental and off the shelf hardware and software to provide world leading communications capability for ADF personnel. It can be rapidly deployed from anywhere, and its modular design provides a flexible, scalable solution that can be customized for various mission needs.
We have a range of opportunities available as we plan for future work. This role will see you applying your Linux skills to either our core product for Army, upgrading the capability to integrate with related maritime communications projects, or modernization of the joint air battlespace management system.
Our team has a unique culture, which prioritises innovation whilst maintaining a culture of flexibility and fun. We are collaborative, adaptable and outcome-focused. You'll find yourself working in an inclusive team, where people really matter. We work flexibly, with a hybrid in office/ work from home arrangement.
Responsibilities

  • Contribute to the full-stack development of innovative networking and communications solutions with a focus on cyber security, scalability and performance.
  • Design, development, integration and test of integrated hardware and software systems.
  • The ability to communicate and collaborate with a diverse team, in a conscientious and inclusive way.
Experience/Qualifications

Essential:
  • Applicants must be Australian Citizens to meet defence security requirements.
  • We invite a diverse range of people to apply, including people from outside of the defence industry.
Desirable:
  • Experience within Defence or complex engineering project environments
  • Linux skills with an understanding of the OS, Sys-admin and Virtualization
  • Understanding of network security, PKI and IP networking protocols
  • Experience in building secure applications in a Linux environment
