The Role: Embedded Software Engineer

Do you thrive on pushing the boundaries of electronics? Join us in an exciting role where your passion meets innovation. As an Embedded Software Engineer, you'll be at the forefront of firmware development, testing, and documentation. Dive into the challenge of crafting digital control systems for heavy-duty rotating equipment, used in power generation applications spanning diesel, gas, and steam turbines.

What You'll Do:

  • Innovate : Develop and maintain embedded software using C, breathing life into cutting-edge technologies.
  • Solve : Uncover the mysteries of embedded software, including debugging and fault finding.
  • Connect : Craft communication interfaces (Ethernet, CAN, Serial) that keep industries running seamlessly.
  • Contribute : Be part of the full product development life cycle, participate in our legacy of excellence.
  • Document : Maintain accurate and up-to-date documentation, a vital component of our quality commitment.
  • Collaborate : Join forces with a small tight-knit team of engineers, fostering ongoing support and growth.

About You:

  • Education : Hold a relevant tertiary qualification in Electronics, Electrical, Computer Engineering or equivalent, as a foundation for your journey.
  • Experience : Flaunt your firmware development expertise with a minimum of 1+ year experience.
  • Code : Boast a strong grasp of embedded software development, turning ideas into functional reality.
  • Communication : Your communication, organisational, and documentation skills set you apart.
  • Team Spirit : Collaboration is your middle name - you thrive as an integral part of a successful team.

Desirable:

  • Experience with Functional Safety SIL1, SIL2 rated software and the IEC61508 product development process is advantageous. (IEC61508 or similar)
  • You have experience in writing software for real-time and distributed systems.
  • Experience with FPGA programming. You're one step ahead in this game-changing field.
  • You are comfortable with reading electrical schematics and datasheets.
