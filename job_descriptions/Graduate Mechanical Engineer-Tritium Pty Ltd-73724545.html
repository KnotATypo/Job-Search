About Tritium

Founded in 2001, Tritium (NASDAQ: DCFC) designs and manufactures proprietary hardware and software to create advanced and reliable DC fast chargers for electric vehicles. Tritium’s compact and robust chargers are designed to look great on Main Street and thrive in harsh conditions, through technology engineered to be easy to install, own, and use. Tritium is focused on continuous innovation in support of our customers around the world.

About the Role

The Graduate Mechanical Engineer will be responsible for the supporting the design and development of mechanical components and systems, throughout the full product development lifecycle.
  • Build stable working relationships with multi-disciplinary, cross-functional teams – Mechanical, Manufacturing, Quality, Electrical, Electronic, and Software/Firmware Engineers, Industrial Designers, and Supply Chain and Production personnel.
  • Follow standard Tritium Product Development practices and procedures.
  • Stay up to date with industry trends and emerging technologies related to Electric Vehicle Supply Equipment (EVSE).
  • Develop sound understanding of low and high-volume manufacturing methods i.e., sheet metal fabrication, press/stamp metal, CNC milling/turning, 3D printing, injection moulding, die-casting etc.
  • Develop sound understanding of “Lean Six Sigma” framework and “V-Model” development cycle.
  • Develop sound understanding of the high-volume assembly line manufacturing process.
  • Conduct research and literature reviews of topics/technologies specific to sub-system and/or components.
  • Interpret system, sub-system, and component requirements and appropriate standards.
  • Support the Mechanical Product Dev. Team with the development of concepts for sub-systems and/or components.
  • Perform hand/1D calculations and use Computer Aided Engineering (CAE) i.e., FEA, CFD.
  • Support the Mechanical Product Dev. Team with detailed design and supporting documentation i.e., drawings, and/or Bill of Materials (BOM), for sub-systems and/or components.
  • Support the Mechanical Product Dev. Team with the development of Cost of Goods Sold (CoGS).
  • Support the Mechanical Product Dev. Team with drafting of test plans for validation testing of sub-system and/or components.
  • Support the Support the Mechanical Product Dev. Team and/or Engineering Technical Services (ETS) Team with conducting validation testing of sub-system and/or components.
    Support the Mechanical Product Dev. Team with New Product Integration (NPI) tasks.
  • Proactively ask questions and reach out for help when “stuck”.
  • Voice concerns or “need for clarification” to line manager.

  • Experience with 3D parametric modeling software (proficiency in SolidWorks is desirable).

  • Familiarity (experience desirable) with low and/or high-volume manufacturing methods i.e., sheet metal fabrication, press/stamp metal, CNC milling/turning, 3D printing, injection moulding, die-casting etc.
