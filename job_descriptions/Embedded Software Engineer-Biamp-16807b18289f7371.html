Full job description

The role, at a glance:
Biamp is seeking an intelligent and highly motivated Software Engineer to design, implement, test, debug, and maintain embedded software for networked digital audiovisual processing equipment and related accessory products. You'll have a handprint in creating world-class audiovisual experiences for our customers around the world. Without realizing it, you have probably already experienced high-quality audio and video emanating from Biamp equipment installed worldwide.

If you have a drive for picking up new technologies and solving new engineering problems, with a genuine passion for Embedded Software or Firmware Engineering, we enthusiastically encourage you to apply.

How you'll contribute:

  • Analyze product requirements to determine necessary data structures and behaviors.
  • Create written documentation for software designs and APIs.
  • Design and implement object-oriented embedded software modules using UML, and standard design patterns and techniques.
  • Implement primarily in modern C++, with some Python, Typescript and C.
  • Target primarily Linux on ARM, and occasionally FreeRTOS and bare-metal.
  • Define and write unit tests which effectively validate module designs.
  • Help in the design, development, and support of common technologies, tools, and processes that will be used to build new Biamp products.
  • Interact with the broader engineering and product management organizations, reaching out when blocked, proactively seeking the information you need to get your job done.
  • Other duties and implementation in other languages such as Python and Typescript as assigned.

A successful candidate should have:

  • A degree in Engineering or a similar discipline, with a strong understanding of Computer Science
  • A working knowledge of C++11 upwards
  • A strong understanding of concurrency, coherence, synchronization, and communication in a multi-threaded, multi-process and distributed environments
  • Familiarity with object-oriented programming
  • Familiarity with the full software development lifecycle, with an appreciation for the collaborative benefits of design and review
  • An ability to debug complex problems
  • An ability to take ownership of unit testing your work and identify ways to automate and reuse
  • A continuous improvement mindset with a desire to apply your skills in new and interesting ways
  • Excellent verbal and written communication skills in English

Some nice-to-haves:

  • Knowledge of Python and Typescript
  • Knowledge of Git source control
  • Knowledge of Yocto, Linux user- and kernal-space, TCP/IP networking, USB, digital audio and video protocols, Xilinx-Zynq, ARM, and FreeRTOS
  • Knowledge of the electronics to firmware interface around microprocessors, memory, and various I/O
