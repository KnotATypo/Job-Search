Jem Health is a successful and growing local company that aims to provide high quality pre-employment / occupational health assessment services to our clients with an overarching goal to promote a safe work place. Through our consistent quality, evidence based assessments and caring about what we do, we help both the individual prospective employees, and the employing company to reduce their risk of harm in the workplace.

THE OPPORTUNITY

An opportunity has presented for a highly motivated and enthusiastic Exercise Physiologist or Physiotherapist to join the Jem Health team as an Occupational Health Assessor (OHA). Your role will be to provide exceptional service, consistent quality and attention to detail in the delivery of employment-related health assessments and occupational health initiatives.

We are currently seeking applications for our Spring Hill, QLD office. We can be flexible and work around study, second jobs and home commitments for the right applicant(s) and are open to a full time role or a couple of part time roles.

Jem Health, Health Assessor staff are responsible for the following key areas of responsibility (not limited to):

-     Occupational Health Assessments including pre-employment medicals, audiometry, spirometry, ECGs, drug screening, functional capacity assessments and fitness testing.

-      Timely and accurate reporting

-      High level compliance and observation of privacy act

-      Worker health checks and ergonomics

-      General administration

-      Monitoring and maintaining stock levels & equipment

-      Health and wellness projects, initiatives, training and promotion

-      Contribute to client relationship management, continuous improvement and business growth initiatives

-      Commitment and compliance to strong safety culture

  • Bachelor degree in Health Science, Physio or Ex Phys or similar health discipline
  • High interest in occupational health
  • Registration/accreditation with AHPRA / ESSA
  • Current and maintained First Aid and CPR
  • Exceptional interpersonal skills and demonstrated ability to build rapport
  • Intermediate to strong computer skills including demonstrated proficiency in Microsoft Office Suite and using two screens.
  • Advanced written and verbal communication skills
  • Willingness to learn and grow
  • Team contributor
  • High level of professionalism
  • A very high level of care and attention to detail
  • Exceptional time management, organisation and ability to work autonomously & as a team.
  • High level of emotional intelligence

Desirable but not essential Criteria:

  • Experience/exposure to Occupational Health
  • Certificate IV or Diploma in WHS
  • Experience or Qualification in Audiometry
  • Experience or Qualification in Spirometry
  • Experience or Qualification in Drug and Alcohol Screening
  • Drivers licence

All applicants must have the right to work in Australia
