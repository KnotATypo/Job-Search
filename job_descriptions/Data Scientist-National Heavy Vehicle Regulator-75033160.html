About the Role

This an exciting opportunity to join the National Heavy Vehicle Regulator’s Data Science and Analytics unit, where we make a real difference to road safety. We are integral to driving better safety outcomes for the heavy vehicle industry and reducing fatalities and injuries on our road.

At a high level, you will help deliver productivity to the industry by designing advanced data algorithms to provide insights from our data platform and expose new opportunities. You will code and test the algorithms to produce an answer that we expect or don't expect.

Our Data Scientists use machine learning techniques to demonstrate statistical significance within our data. We use Agile project environment to undertake research and development through coding.

NHVR offers a supportive hybrid work environment with flexible work from home arrangements . We encourage work-life balance and celebrate additional leave through rostered days off and an additional week off over the Christmas/New Year period.

This is a permanent position based in our Brisbane office within Newstead’s Gasworks precinct – close to cafes and transport, and access to EOT facilities. We also welcome applications from candidates in Melbourne and Sydney.

About You

You will hold a tertiary qualification in Data Science, Computer Science, Statistics or Applied Mathematics, or 5 years’ experience in Data Science. You will have experience using a data science platform such as Microsoft Fabric or Snowflake (Databricks experience is a bonus!).

Experience with Machine Learning and building production pipelines is essential for the role. You will have knowledge of statistical techniques and concepts, and possess good written skills to accurately document our systems and the outcomes.

Good communication skills are key to engaging frequently with other Data Scientists and Data Engineers, and providing expertise in mathematical and statistical concepts to stakeholders across the business.

The successful candidate will be innovative, have a strong sense of motivation, and be able to engage in problem-solving and lateral thinking.

About Us

The National Heavy Vehicle Regulator (NHVR) commenced operations in 2013 as the national entity responsible for regulating all vehicles in Australia over 4.5 tonnes gross vehicle mass. The NHVR minimises the compliance burden on the heavy vehicle transport industry, reduces duplication and inconsistences across state and territory borders, and ultimately for the heavy vehicle business with government in Australia.

We are headquartered in Brisbane and employ more than 750 people across the ACT, New South Wales, Queensland, South Australia, Tasmania and Victoria.
