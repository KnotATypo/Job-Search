Assist in the controlled release of bodies; and preparation of deceased for viewing and identification purposes, and provide assistance to the pathologist with photography of deceased and collection, labelling and processing of specimens.

Key competencies (role specific criteria)

You will be assessed on your ability to demonstrate, or rapidly acquire the skills to demonstrate the following:

  • Pathology laboratory experience with ability to follow both written and oral instruction and identify and solve problems methodically.
  • Reliability, integrity, and professional attitude necessary for a medico-legal and potentially hazardous workplace.
  • A high level computer and information technology skills with the ability to learn and use Auslab (case management system), QIS2 (quality system) and Microsoft software or equivalent computer applications within a mortuary environment.
  • Understanding of relevant legislation and guidelines and their application to a department undertaking medico-legal autopsies.
  • Sound judgement, ability to think critically, effective communication and interpersonal skills, with the proven ability to work in a team and contribute positively to team outcomes.
  • Ability to proactively maintain a safe, secure and clean working environment and an understanding of and willingness to contribute to the relevant systems, processes and procedures.
