Company Description

Gameloft Brisbane , part of the global Gameloft family, opened in 2014 and since then has released Zombie Anarchy, Ballistic Baseball, My Little Pony: Mane Merge, and multi-award-winning The Oregon Trail™. In 2022, Gameloft Brisbane was announced as the winner of the coveted ‘Studio of the Year’ at the Australian Game Developer Awards (AGDAs).

We believe the keys to success are a great studio culture and finding a balance in everything we do, whether it's working with great people on exciting projects or enjoying the incredible lifestyle of Queensland Australia.

We're setting our sights on an exciting new direction in game development, embracing the challenges and opportunities of cross-platform innovation. As pioneers in leading Brisbane's game development landscape, we're dedicated to crafting immersive experiences that transcend traditional boundaries and engage players across diverse platforms. As we venture into this exciting phase, we're also expanding our talented team to bring to life a range of unannounced projects.

Job Description

As a Gameplay Programmer, you will be joining a highly collaborative and talented team and be responsible for prototyping and production of new games for mobile and other platforms, and will work with well-known licences!

Responsibilities

  • Create gameplay features in our new and exciting projects
  • Work closely and collaboratively with designers to create detailed feature specifications
  • Take ownership of gameplay features implementation
  • Design and implement innovative solutions to solve creative and technical challenges
  • Write clean, robust and reusable code and create documentation where required; debug, diagnose and fix code issues
  • Rapidly prototype and write final code
  • Independently use documentation and guides to complete work or overcome impediments
  • Take active role in providing time estimates and task breakdowns
  • Provide feedback to other programmers and mentor junior programmers as appropriate

Qualifications

  • Computer Science or similar degree, or equivalent professional experience
  • Minimum of 3 years of experience in games development
  • Solid understanding of modern C++; experience with C# and Python is advantageous
  • Experience with commercial game engines, preferable Unreal Engine
  • Experience with a modern source version-control system (Git, Subversion or Perforce)
  • Effective communicator with great interpersonal skills
