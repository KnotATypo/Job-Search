Position Overview

One of QLD's largest state departments are seeking an electrical engineer who is eager to learn & better this skills! In this role, you'll play a vital part in ensuring the efficiency, safety, and functionality of traffic signals, road lighting, and Intelligent Transport Systems. You'll collaborate with a dynamic team to plan, design, construct, and maintain these systems. The department are looking for someone with limited experienced & someone with a willingness to learn in the graduate space!

Key Responsibilities
  • Develop and test configuration files for traffic signal controllers in line with design specifications and organizational policies.
  • Participate in project management activities, contributing to the planning, design, and construction phases of electrical and electronic projects.
  • Assist in preparing design processes to ensure compliance and successful project outcomes.
  • Apply problem-solving skills and professional judgment to analyze and conceptualize solutions to meet design objectives.
  • Identify potential issues and negotiate adjustments to design briefs as necessary for project acceptance.
  • Stay updated on relevant standards, policies, and legislative requirements related to electrical, electronic, and IT systems in road infrastructure deployment.
  • Liaise with internal and external stakeholders on matters related to road infrastructure development.
  • Develop leadership and management skills through on-the-job opportunities.
  • Uphold ethical standards and adhere to legislative frameworks and workplace health & safety policies.

Mandatory Requirements
  • Bachelor's degree in Electrical, Electronics, Mechatronics, or Software Engineering from a recognized institution.

Role/Occupational Capabilities
  • Demonstrated understanding of ITS, Road Lighting, and Traffic Signals principles, processes, and standards.
  • Customer-focused mindset with the ability to engage stakeholders respectfully and effectively.
  • Proactive approach to tasks, seeking clarity, organizing priorities, and reporting progress.
  • Commitment to personal development and continuous improvement, including self-reflection and learning from experiences.
