About us:

Lockheed Martin is a dynamic, growing, and energetic organisation offering stimulating, challenging work at the forefront of technology. Lockheed Martin is a leader in Australia’s National Security, and our collaboration with Australian industry spans the Aeronautics, Maritime, Surveillance and Defence domains. We employ approximately 114,000 staff worldwide, and over 1200 staff in Australia across multiple locations.
Following a recent announcement by the Australian Government to investigate the acquisition of UH-60M Black Hawk helicopters from the US Government, Lockheed Martin Australia is looking to establish a core team of experienced military Rotary Wing professionals to support the potential procurement and sustainment activities. The modern-day, 3rd Generation UH-60M Black Hawk helicopter is the world’s leading multi-role battlefield and special operations helicopter with a proven legacy of exceptional capability and reliability. Lockheed Martin Australia is looking forward to being the key contributor to the Australian UH-60M Black Hawk program and encourages you to come join us in delivering a sovereign sustainment capability.
We are pleased to invite applications for the position of Software Assurance SME to join our UH-60M Black Hawk team.

Your responsibilities will include:

  • Working in collaboration with the System Safety Program Manager - UH-60M, the Software Assurance SME will:
  • Identify and review Software Assurance Requirements associated with proposed changes as part of Software System Requirements Hazard Analysis (SRHA).
  • Provide SME input to Functional Hazard Analysis (FHA) as and when required, including the provision of advice associated with any potential for Systematic Common Mode Failures.
  • Provide SME input to Sub-System Hazard Analysis (SSHA) and System Hazard Analysis (SHA) activities as and when required.
  • Evaluate the System Software validation and verification risk mitigation measures, and management of associated risks in support of test events.
  • Evaluate the of Engineering Change Proposals (ECPs), Change Notices (CNs), Deficiency Reports, Mishaps, and Requests for Deviations and Waivers from a Software Assurance perspective.
  • Define tests and demonstrations, or in using other verification methods on safety significant software functionality to verify compliance with safety requirements.
  • Oversee and ensure that all Software Assurance activities are aligned with the requirements of the Airworthiness Design Requirements Manual, Section 2, Chapters 2 & 3 ‘System Safety’ & Aviation Software’ respectively.
  • Guide the management of all software load control and criticality lists in-line with ‘Defence Aviation Safety Program Manual, Volume 2: Defence Aviation Safety Regulation (DASR): DASR 21 – Aircraft
  • Design, Production and Certification: Section A: Technical Requirements: SUBPART G: Military Production Organisation Approval: 21.A.165 – Obligations of the holder’.
  • Ensure all Software Assurance Continuing Airworthiness
  • Management Obligations (CAMO) obligations as defined by the ‘Defence Aviation Safety Program Manual, Volume 2: Defence Aviation Safety Regulation (DASR): DASR M – Continuing Airworthiness Management: Section A: SUBPART B: Accountability: M.A.201 – Responsibilities’.
To be successful in this role, you will have:
  • Significant evidence of formally acquired knowledge and experience in the application of Functional Hazard Analysis (FHA) techniques in support of the derivation of Software Assurance Requirements.
  • Significant evidence of formally acquired knowledge and experience in the application of recognised industry standards that support software safety assurance e.g. RTCA DO-178C and IEC 61508. Including understanding of varying levels of assurance required for varying levels of Software Criticality covering: • Levels of Requisite Planning
  • Application of Development Processes
  • Software Requirements Validation & Verification
  • Levels of Testing
  • Configuration Management • Software Quality Assurance
  • Software Certification.
Desirable
  • Exposure to Defence Aviation System Safety Programs / Software Development Programs.

About you:

The Software Assurance SME provides the UH-60M System Safety Program Manager with subject matter advice (SME) on submitted Software Assurance artefacts, thus discharging their associated obligations as a Software Assurance Compliance Verification Engineer (CVE), and as a member a defined Military Type Certificate Holder (MTCH).
